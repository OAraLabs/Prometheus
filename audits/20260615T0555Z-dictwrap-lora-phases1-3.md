## Phase 2 ‚Äî Train ‚õî BLOCKED (hardware/library, precisely characterized)
The brand-new `gemma4` arch loads fine under `AutoModelForCausalLM` (the big arch risk is gone),
but it does not fit one 24 GB card for training:

| attempt | result |
|---|---|
| `device_map="auto"` | over-conservative ‚Üí tried to CPU-offload at load |
| `device_map={"":0}` (all GPU) | **OOM by ~1 GB** ‚Äî the "4-bit" model is **~22 GB resident** |
| `expandable_segments` | no change (~22 GB is real, not fragmentation) |
| CPU-offload experts | `Params4bit.__new__` rejects accelerate's `_is_hf_initialized` (patched) |
| ‚Ä¶after patch | `Tensor.item() cannot be called on meta tensors` (offload ‚Üî trl) |

**Root cause:** bnb 4-bit quantizes `nn.Linear` modules, but gemma4's **128 MoE experts are
fused tensors** it skips ‚Üí they stay bf16 ‚Üí ~22 GB resident, no training headroom. CPU-offloading
the frozen experts is the right architecture but hits an accelerate‚Üîbnb‚Üîtransformers
incompatibility chain on this arch.

**This corrects Phase 0.** The Phase-0 "bitsandbytes 4-bit works ‚Üí QLoRA viable on one 4090"
gate was validated on a **toy `Params4bit` tensor**, not the real 26B MoE multimodal load. The
toy test passed; the real model doesn't fit. Attention-only LoRA targeting (q/k/v/o) was the
correct call ‚Äî the blocker is the frozen base size, not the LoRA target.
